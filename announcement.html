What New Members Can Expect

Community marching band members can expect a variety of experiences and opportunities, including:

  • Rehearsals: Members can expect regular rehearsals to learn and perfect music, marching techniques, and formations. These rehearsals often occur weekly or bi-weekly, depending on the band's schedule and commitments.
  • Performances: Community marching bands typically perform at various events such as parades, festivals, sporting events, and community gatherings. Members can expect to participate in these performances, showcasing their skills and entertaining audiences.
  • Teamwork: Being part of a marching band requires strong teamwork and camaraderie. Members can expect to develop close bonds with their fellow musicians and work together to achieve a unified performance.
  • Skill Development: Joining a marching band offers opportunities for skill development in music, marching, and performance. Members can expect to improve their musical abilities, coordination, and discipline through regular practice and performances.
  • Travel: Some community marching bands may have opportunities to travel to different cities or even countries to participate in competitions, festivals, or special events. Members can expect to experience new places and cultures through these travel opportunities.
  • Community Involvement: Community marching bands often play a significant role in local events and celebrations. Members can expect to become actively involved in their community, representing their band with pride and contributing to the cultural fabric of their area.
  • Fun and Enjoyment: Ultimately, being part of a marching band is about having fun and enjoying the experience of making music with others. Members can expect to create lasting memories, forge friendships, and find fulfillment in their musical endeavors.

Band Practice Essentials

Members should have the following items for the band camp and the upcoming season:

  1. Personal water cooler or hydration backpack (i.e. Coleman Cooler or Camelbak)
  2. Athletic shoes (no sandals)
  3. Sunglasses
  4. Ball cap or wide-brimmed hat
  5. Khakis shorts (in good condition—no tears or rips)
  6. Sunscreen and bug repellant
  7. Comfortable clothes for outdoor rehearsals
  8. Alarm clock
  9. Any medication you may need

COVID-19 Prevention Measures

As we continue to navigate through the challenges presented by the COVID-19 pandemic, the health and safety of our community and our members remain our top priority. We must all do our part to prevent the spread of the virus and protect ourselves and others. Here are some important guidelines and measures that we must adhere to:

  1. Mask Requirement: Masks will be required for all indoor rehearsals, meetings, and performances, regardless of vaccination status. Masks should fully cover the nose and mouth and be worn at all times when indoors, except when actively playing a wind instrument.
  2. Social Distancing: Whenever possible, we will maintain a safe distance of at least six feet between members during rehearsals and performances. This includes spacing out while marching and avoiding congregating in large groups before and after events.
  3. Hand Hygiene: Proper hand hygiene is crucial in preventing the spread of the virus. We encourage all members to regularly wash their hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds or use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol.
  4. Stay Home if Sick: If you are experiencing any symptoms of illness, including fever, cough, sore throat, or loss of taste or smell, please stay home and refrain from attending rehearsals or performances until you are fully recovered.
  5. Vaccination and Testing: We strongly encourage all eligible members to receive the COVID-19 vaccine to protect themselves and others. Additionally, we may implement periodic testing for members, especially before important events or travel.
  6. Respect Venue Policies: When performing at external venues, we will adhere to any additional COVID-19 guidelines and protocols implemented by the organizers or hosts. This may include wearing masks, providing proof of vaccination or negative test results, or adhering to capacity limits.
  7. Flexibility and Adaptability: As the situation evolves, we must remain flexible and adapt our plans and procedures accordingly. We will continue to monitor guidance from health authorities and adjust our practices as needed to ensure the safety of our members and community.

By following these guidelines and working together, we can help mitigate the spread of COVID-19 and continue to enjoy the experience of making music together as a community marching band. Thank you for your cooperation and commitment to keeping each other safe.

Auditions

All applicants of the Cavite Cavaliers DBC are required to perform an audition. These auditions are not used to exclude you from joining the band but are necessary to help assign you to the part that best matches your abilities.

Success Checklist

  1. Complete the Drill Spot Reservation Form. Be sure to click the “submit” button once you’ve carefully entered your information. This must be completed by Friday, March 22.
  2. Register for the marching band class.
  3. Be sure your instrument is in good working order and you have plenty of reeds, valve oil, etc. Large instruments including, mellophone, euphonium/baritone, contrabass, timpani, drums, and concert percussion will be provided, all others must be brought by the members.
  4. Put all event dates on your calendar. Stay tuned for any changes.
  5. Bring all items on the Band Practice Essentials list.
  6. Prepare your part-placement audition music.
